The Relief Catalouge.

French Country

Relief Interior.

French country is a very interesting style that can give warm, cozy, traditional vibes while keeping the space very much modern. You want to select a warm, earthy color palette to begin with, such as red, yellow, and gold.

Bohemian Design

Relief Interior

Bohemian is a style that interests many people. Essential, it’s super carefree and relaxed – there are very few rules to follow when emulating this style.

Shaby-Chick

Relief Interior

Shabby chic is a vintage-inspired style that you can think of as soft, feminine, and delicate. It has lots of distressed furnishings, antique style paint, and white, cream, and pastel color schemes. Now, although this interior design style had its heyday a few years ago, many enjoy for comforting style and accessories.

Scandinavian (hygge)

Relief Interior

Scandinavian style, also known as “hygge,” is another popular trend that you’ve likely spotted on sites like Pinterest or Instagram. This style aims to channel the simplicity of life demonstrated in Nordic countries.

MAINSTREAM DESIGNS

Contemprary Design

Relief Interior.

Contemporary interior design is the most futuristic and progressive decor type, with a focus on innovative form factor, refinement in simplicity, and adaptability. Taking cues from its cosmopolitan environment.

Transitional Design

Relief Interior

Transitional interior design style is a mix between modern and classical decor aesthetics, often involving a cleaner and simpler look than classical, but still keeping some of its embellishments and detailing.

Industrial Design

Relief Interior

Industrial style decor is a look that hearkens back to the turn-of-the-century industrial era. It emphasizes liberal use of exposed steel with distressed wooden elements.

Traditional Design

Relief Interior

Traditional style interior design is a decor type that draws inspiration from classical, pre-1920s furnishing and architecture, with a focus on elegant and ornate furnishing. In the North American context.
